The What

Ejaculation occurs when a man climaxes (cums) during sexual activity and the semen is ejected from the penis. Ejaculation is the medical term for climaxing (cuming).

Is lovemaking over faster than you would like?

Are you bothered by your lack of control?

Premature Ejaculation may be the problem.

What is premature ejaculation?

Premature ejaculation (PE) is a very common condition that affects about 1 in every 3 men.

Men with PE ejaculate sooner than they would like during sexual intercourse. Men with severe PE may ejaculate before penetration occurs.

Men with PE usually feel that they cannot control when they ejaculate and are bothered or distressed by this situation.

What are the consequences of the condition?

Many men with this issue feel embarrassed and upset. They may not be satisfied with their sexual performance and worry that their partners are also not satisfied. This can lead to the avoidance of sexual intimacy and also relationship problems.

The STUDY

What is the purpose of this clinical study?

This clinical research study is being performed to test the safety and effectiveness of an investigational drug for PE. It is hoped that this investigational drug will be safe and effective for the treatment for PE.

Studies such as this must be performed in order for the investigational drug to be approved and eventually become available for the treatment of PE.

What is the investigational drug?

The medication being investigated relaxes muscles and has been used safely in many millions of patients globally for a variety of conditions. The medication is not currently approved for use in PE, thus it is considered investigational for this condition.

It is thought that in PE the muscles involved in ejaculation may be over-active. Relaxation of these muscles may therefore be effective in treating the PE.

How will the response to the investigational drug be assessed?

To assess the response, the time that it takes to ejaculate (climax) will be measured on a number of occasions during the study.

These measurements will be done at home by you and your partner using a simple stopwatch. The stopwatch will be provided to you for use in this study.

STUDY PARTICIPATION

Who may participate in the study?

Men who meet the following key criteria may participate in the study:

Aged between 18 and 60

Premature ejaculation has occurred for most of the man’s life (usually since becoming sexually active)

The man has a female partner (in a stable relationship)

There are other criteria that the study team will discuss with you to ensure you are fully eligible to participate in the study.

What Will Happen If I Participate In This Clinical Study?

A detailed explanation of what will happen if you participate will be provided by the study team, and you will have the opportunity to have all of your questions answered. If you decide to participate in the study, you will be assessed to determine if you are fully eligible for the study. If you are eligible, you will receive the investigational drug and will be followed for a minimum of 3 months to assess your response. The investigational drug and all study related care will be provided to you at no cost.

You will need to attend the research clinic/office on several occasions during the 3 month follow-up period (the study team will be able to explain when you should attend). The majority of visits to the research clinic will be short (approximately 1 hour each). You will be compensated for the cost of travel to the clinic.

What Should I Do To Find Out More Information About Participating In This Study?

You will need to contact us (see details below under Contact Details).

You will then be invited to a study clinic to discuss the study further with members of the research team. (The initial discussion may occur on the phone also) It will be determined if you are eligible to participate in the study. You will be provided further details on:

  • premature ejaculation

  • the investigational drug

  • the requirements to participate in the study

  • the assessments during the study

  • any other topics you may want to discuss.

You will be compensated for the cost of your travel to the clinic. Please discuss with the study staff.

Does my partner need to participate?

Your female partner will need to attend at least one visit to the clinic/office or videoconference in order to have the study explained and to be trained on the use of the stopwatch. The research team will show both of you how to use the stopwatch and record the results. It will not be necessary for your partner to attend any further visits.

Where Is The Study Being Performed?

The study is being performed in the USA and UK. All the study teams are led by doctors experienced in conducting clinical research of premature ejaculation. Men with premature ejaculation (PE) from anywhere in the USA or UK can participate in the study.

How Do I Get More Information?

Please contact us to find out more information about the study and to see if you are eligible to participate. Asking for more information about the study does not mean that you are willing to participate. Before you make the final decision to participate in the study, you and your partner will be provided detailed written and verbal information and be given the opportunity to have all of your questions answered. We look forward to hearing from you.
